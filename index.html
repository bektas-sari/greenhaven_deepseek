About Greenhaven

Our History

Founded in 1892 as a small agricultural settlement, Greenhaven has grown into a thriving city of 85,000 residents while maintaining its connection to nature. Our tree-lined streets and numerous parks pay homage to our roots while we embrace modern development.

In 2023, Greenhaven was recognized as one of the "Top 10 Most Livable Small Cities" in the country, a testament to our balanced approach to growth and community values.

Mission & Vision

Mission: To serve our community with integrity, innovation, and inclusivity, fostering a sustainable and prosperous city for all.

Vision: To be a model 21st century municipality that harmonizes economic vitality, environmental stewardship, and social equity.

Message from Mayor Sarah Johnson

"Greenhaven's strength lies in our community spirit and commitment to sustainable progress. As your mayor, I'm dedicated to maintaining our city's unique character while ensuring we meet the needs of all residents today and for generations to come."

Explore Greenhaven

Discover the attractions that make our city special

Botanical Gardens

Our 50-acre gardens feature native plants, themed gardens, and seasonal displays that attract visitors year-round.

Riverside Park

This 120-acre park along the Green River offers hiking trails, picnic areas, and stunning views of the water.

Farmers Market

Every Saturday from May to October, our award-winning market features local produce, crafts, and live music.

Historic Downtown

Our beautifully preserved downtown features unique shops, restaurants, and architecture from the early 20th century.

Museum of Art

This regional art museum showcases rotating exhibitions and a permanent collection focusing on local artists.

Nature Preserve

Over 300 acres of protected land with diverse ecosystems, offering educational programs and nature trails.
